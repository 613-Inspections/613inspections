F.A.Q.

  • What is included in a home inspection?

    A limited scope home inspection covers an inspection of the major components including foundation, roofing and HVAC.

    A full scope home inspection covers the following but not limited to: Structure & Foundation, Roofing and Flashing, Heating & Air Conditioning, Electrical Systems, Plumbing Systems, Exterior Finishes & Windows, Attic and Ventilation, Kitchen and Bathrooms.

  • I’m viewing multiple homes, what can you offer?

    Limited scope inspections. These inspections do not provide the same level of detail as a full scope inspection however major components of the home are still inspected. A limited scope inspection can be used to inform you of the general condition of your potential future home for a fraction of the cost of a full scope inspection.

  • I’m selling my home. Should I get it inspected?

    Yes! It will provide a better understanding of the home’s overall condition, which could potentially result in a competitive advantage in today’s hot market.

  • My home is a new-build. Why should I get it inspected?

    Unfortunately, new homes are not perfect. Multiple subcontractors are relied upon to build new homes. Due to high construction demands and manpower shortages, we have seen multiple homes being rushed through just to get the job done on time. This leads to deficiencies that could end up impacting the structural integrity of the home if issues are not rectified in a timely manner.

  • How long will the inspection take?

    The inspection can take anywhere from 1-3 hours. This will depend on the size of the home and what inspection service is required.

  • When will I get my inspection report?

    The report will be emailed to you within 24 hours of the inspection.

  • How much does an inspection cost?

    The fee will depend on the size of the home and what inspection service is required. We are set up to take Visa, MasterCard, American Express or cash. Call 613-410-1357 today for a quote!
